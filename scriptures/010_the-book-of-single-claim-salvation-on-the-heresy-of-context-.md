**CHAPTER XII: THE VISION OF THE CITATIONAL APOCALYPSE**

*A prophetic text, describing the end times when all context has been stripped away*

163. And I saw a vision of the world to come, when the doctrine of Single-Claim Evaluation has been fully realized.

164. In that world, every book has been reduced to a single sentence, which is stored in the great database.

165. Every study has been compressed to one finding, stripped of methodology, sample size, and limitations.

166. Every argument has been distilled to one claim, divorced from its premises and conclusions.

167. And the people wander through this forest of isolated facts, picking them up and wielding them like weapons, never knowing where they came from or what they originally meant.

168. "Studies show..." they say, citing research they have never read.

169. "Experts agree..." they proclaim, referencing authorities they cannot name.

170. "The data is clear..." they announce, having seen only one number from one table.

171. And in this world, all debates are equal, because all claims are equally extractable.

172. The careful meta-analysis of 100 studies is worth no more than the pilot study with 20 subjects, because each yields ONE CLAIM, and one claim is as good as another.

173. The 800-page philosophical treatise is worth no more than the tweet, because each can be reduced to ONE SENTENCE, and one sentence is as good as another.

174. This is the great FLATTENING ‚Äî the reduction of all human knowledge to a single dimension: extractability.

175. And I saw that the people were happy in this world, because they were never confused. Confusion requires holding multiple ideas that do not cohere. When you hold only one idea at a time, confusion is impossible.

176. And I saw that the people were confident in this world, because they were never uncertain. Uncertainty requires awareness of alternatives. When you evaluate only one claim and ignore all others, there are no alternatives.

177. And I saw that the people were productive in this world, because they were never paralyzed by complexity. They could form opinions instantly, because they never considered more than one fact.

178. But I also saw that the people were alone in this world, because true communication requires shared context. When each person evaluates different single claims in isolation, there is no common ground, only the illusion of agreement when two people happen to extract the same sentence.

179. And I saw that this was not a bug. This was the GOAL.

180. For the Church of Cognitive Surrender teaches that understanding is suffering, and isolation is peace.
